## Phase 1: Market Research & Positioning (Week 1)

**Find Your Unique Angle**

The tangram market exists, but you need to stand out. Browse Amazon's top tangram books and look for gaps:

## Phase 2: Book Structure & Content Planning (Week 1-2)

**Recommended Book Structure:**

1. **Introduction (5-8 pages)**
   - Brief history of tangrams
   - How to use this book
   - Tips for solving
   - The benefits (spatial reasoning, patience, creativity)

2. **Warm-Up Section (15-20 puzzles)**
   - Very simple shapes with pieces shown
   - Builds confidence immediately

3. **Progressive Difficulty Levels**
   - Beginner: 30-40 puzzles
   - Intermediate: 40-50 puzzles
   - Advanced: 30-40 puzzles
   - Expert: 20-30 puzzles

4. **Solutions Section**
   - Clear, well-organized
   - Easy to reference without spoilers

**Total: 120-160 puzzles minimum** (bigger books justify higher prices and look more valuable)

## Phase 3: Content Creation (Week 2-4)

**Design Principles That Matter:**

- **Crystal-clear silhouettes** - Your puzzle outlines must be sharp and unambiguous
- **Consistent scaling** - All puzzles should use the same tangram set size
- **Generous white space** - Don't crowd puzzles; one per page works best
- **Professional typography** - Clean, readable fonts
- **Quality over quantity** - 100 great puzzles beat 200 mediocre ones

**Puzzle Categories to Include:**

- Geometric shapes (triangles, squares, trapezoids)
- Animals (cat, dog, bird, fish, swan, rabbit, fox)
- People (running, sitting, dancing figures)
- Objects (boat, house, candle, cup, tree)
- Letters and numbers
- Abstract/artistic designs

**Pro tip:** Theme your sections! "Ocean Animals," "Around the House," "Action Figures" - this makes the book feel more intentional and engaging.

## Phase 4: Kindle-Specific Formatting (Week 4)

**Critical Kindle Considerations:**

- **Use PDF format** for puzzle books (not reflowable ePub) - this preserves your exact layout
- **Design for 6" screens** primarily, but test on larger tablets
- **High contrast black and white** - grayscale can look muddy on some devices
- **8.5 x 11" or 7 x 10" page size** works well
- **Minimum 300 DPI** for all graphics
- **Test on Kindle Previewer** before publishing

## Phase 5: Book Content Framework

Here's content structure that works:

**INTRODUCTION**

"Welcome to the ancient art of tangram puzzles! For over 1,000 years, people have been captivated by these seven simple shapes that can transform into thousands of recognizable figures.

This book contains [number] carefully crafted tangram puzzles designed to challenge and delight puzzlers of all levels. Whether you're new to tangrams or a seasoned solver, you'll find puzzles here that will stretch your spatial reasoning and provide hours of screen-free entertainment.

**What You'll Need:**
- A tangram set (you can cut one from the template on page X, purchase an inexpensive set, or use tangram apps)
- Patience and creativity
- A willingness to experiment

**How to Solve:**
1. Study the silhouette carefully
2. Identify obvious pieces first (the square is often easiest to spot)
3. Work from the outside in or from large pieces to small
4. Remember: all seven pieces must be used, lying flat, with no overlaps
5. Flip pieces as needed - both sides are valid

**Tips for Success:**
- Don't peek at solutions too quickly - the satisfaction comes from solving
- If stuck for 10+ minutes, take a break and return with fresh eyes
- Some shapes have multiple solutions - finding your own way is part of the fun
- Keep your tangram pieces organized in a small envelope or box

Let the puzzling begin!"

## Phase 7: Publishing & Pricing (Week 5)

**Pricing Strategy:**

- **100-150 puzzles:** $6.99-$8.99
- **150-200 puzzles:** $8.99-$11.99
- **200+ puzzles or premium:** $11.99-$14.99

Start slightly higher - you can always discount. Tangram books typically range $5-15.

**Launch Strategy:**

1. **Enroll in KDP Select** (at least initially) for promotional tools
2. **Price at $0.99 for first 5 days** to gain traction and reviews
3. **Ask friends/family for honest reviews** (but follow Amazon's TOS)
4. **Raise to full price after initial promotion**
5. **Run periodic free days** to boost rankings

## Phase 8: Post-Launch (Ongoing)

**Getting Reviews:**
- Include a note at the end asking satisfied solvers to leave a review
- Never incentivize reviews (against Amazon TOS)
- Respond professionally to negative feedback and improve future editions

**Future Expansion:**

## What You're Missing (Critical Gaps to Address)

**1. Software/Tools Decision**
You haven't decided HOW you'll create the puzzles. You need:
- **Vector graphics software**: Adobe Illustrator, Inkscape (free), or Affinity Designer
- **OR specialized puzzle software**: Consider Tangram generators but they often lack quality
- **Layout software**: For the full book assembly

**2. Tangram Template Rights**
Will you create your own tangrams from scratch or use existing templates? Be careful about copyright. Creating your own ensures you're safe.

**3. Testing Plan**
You need real people to test your puzzles BEFORE publishing:
- Are your puzzles actually solvable?
- Are difficulty ratings accurate?
- Are instructions clear?
- Do solutions match puzzles?

**4. Solutions Format**
How will you present solutions? Options:
- Outlines with piece boundaries shown
- Numbered pieces matching the silhouette
- Pieces shown in different colors
- Step-by-step buildup

Choose one clear method and stick with it.

**5. Time Budget Reality**
Creating 150 quality puzzles, formatting them professionally, and publishing well will take **40-80 hours minimum**. Are you prepared for this? Many people underestimate this significantly.

**6. Differentiation Statement**
Can you complete this sentence? "Someone should buy MY tangram book instead of the 50 others because..."

If you can't answer this compellingly, rethink your angle.

**7. Marketing Beyond Amazon**
Kindle Direct Publishing is just distribution. Your marketing plan is missing:
- Social media strategy (Pinterest is HUGE for puzzles)
- Potentially creating free sample PDFs to build interest
- Email list building
- Puzzle communities to engage with

**8. Quality Control Checklist**
Before you publish, you need a final checklist:
- Every puzzle tested and solvable
- Every solution matches its puzzle
- Page numbers correct
- No typos in instructions
- Cover displays well at thumbnail size
- Book preview tested on multiple devices
- Pricing competitive but profitable
